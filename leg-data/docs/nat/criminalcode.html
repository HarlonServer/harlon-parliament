Criminal Code of the Federation of Harlon

Staff Enforce Rules, Government Enforce Laws Under the Criminal Code

As fundamental values bind us together, this code is a comprehensive framework to define, deter, and address criminal behaviour.

I. Offences Against the Public Order

1.1 - Treason

The act of attempting to undermine the stability of government through forceful violence to overthrow the government, or by sharing classified information to an agent of a state other than Harlon.  

Maximum Sentence: £10000 Penalty, Expulsion From Public Office For 6 Months

1.2 - Sedition

The act of inciting rebellion and or insurrection against the government by advocating for the use of unlawful methods to achieve political change.

Maximum Sentence: £5000 Penalty, Expulsion From Public Office For 3 Months

1.3 Unlawful Assembly

The act of gathering with others with the intention of committing an illegal act, intimidating the general public, or harassing others.

Maximum Sentence: £1000 Penalty, 10 Minutes Prison

1.4 - Trespassing

When an individual enters someone else’s property without their legal permission, provided that such person has given reasonable notice. Reasonable notice can include having a “no trespassing” sign on the property.

First Offence: £50 Penalty

Second Offence:  £100 Penalty, 5 Minutes Prison

1.5 - Resisting Arrest

The act of intentionally and actively resisting a law enforcement officer's attempt to place an individual under arrest. This can include leaving the server or hiding after an officer has informed you of such detainment.

First Offence: £50 Fine

Second Offence:  £100 Fine, 5 Minutes Prison

II. Offences Against the Administration of Law

2.1 - Corruption

The act of using the powers of public office for personal gain. This can include, but is not limited to; manipulating political processes, securing personal benefits by providing favours, unlawfully embezzling public funds,  or manipulating government records.

Maximum Sentence: £10000 Penalty, Expulsion From Public Office For 6 Months

2.2 - Bribery of Government Officers

The act of offering, giving, receiving, or soliciting something of value; with the intention of influencing the actions or decisions of a government employee in the performance of their official duties.

Maximum Sentence: £10000 Penalty, Expulsion From Public Office For 6 Months

2.3 - Electoral Fraud

When someone undermines the electoral system with the intention of affecting the outcome of an election, they are committing electoral fraud. This can include but is not limited to; rigging ballots, bribing voters, or tampering with the count.

Maximum Sentence: £50000 Penalty, Expulsion From Public Office For 6 Months

2.4 - Perjury

When someone has intentionally provided false information in a judicial proceeding, such as in a court of law or during a deposition, they have committed perjury.

Maximum Sentence: £5000 Penalty, 30 Minutes Prison

2.5 - Disobeying the Court

When someone disobeys a lawful order made by a court of justice, they are to be found in contempt of court. This may also include failing to respond when summoned to a judicial proceeding, or attempting to obstruct the functions of the court.

Per Offence: £500 Penalty

2.6 - Punishment Evasion

The act of intentionally evading punishments made by a court of justice, such as monetary penalties or prison time. Individuals who escape prison may also be guilty of punishment evasion.

Maximum Sentence: £1000 Penalty, 30 Minutes Prison

2.7 - Obstruction of Justice

The act of intentionally interfering with the proper functioning of the legal system by tampering with evidence, obstructing an investigation, influencing witnesses, or otherwise attempting to manipulate the outcome of a trial.

Maximum Sentence: £5000 Penalty, 30 Minutes Prison

III. Offences Against Persons

3.1 - Murder

The act of unlawfully taking another person’s life without legal justification.

Per Offence: £200 Penalty

3.2 - Assault

The act of hitting another person more than once.

Per Offence: £100 Penalty

3.3 - Intimidation

The act of threatening to harm another person in order to attempt to compel them to do something against their will.

Per Offence: £50 Penalty

3.4 - False Reporting

The act of knowingly providing untrue information or making false claims about someone's involvement in a crime or wrongdoing.

Maximum Sentence: £5000 Penalty

IV. Commerce and Trade Offences

4.1 - Commercial Fraud

The act of deceiving others in business transactions, contracts, or dealings with the goal of obtaining financial benefits through false representation, manipulation, or a concealment of facts.

Maximum Sentence: £20000 Penalty, Seizure of Stolen Funds, 30 Minutes Prison

4.2 - Money Laundering

The act of concealing proceeds earned through crime via the use of business transactions or dealings.

Maximum Sentence: £5000 Penalty, Seizure of Unlawful Funds, 30 Minutes Prison

4.3 - Identity Fraud
The act of unlawfully using another person's identity for fraudulent purposes, typically with the intent to deceive, gain financial benefits, or engage in illegal activity.

Maximum Sentence: £5000 Penalty, 30 Minutes Prison

4.4 - Tax Evasion
The act of intentionally evading the payment of taxes that a person or entity owes to the government.

Maximum Sentence: £5000 Penalty, 30 Minutes Prison

4.5 - Corporate Espionage

The intentional and unlawful gathering of valuable confidential or proprietary information from one company to benefit another, often for a competitive advantage or financial gain.

Maximum Sentence: £10000 Penalty

4.6 - Market Manipulation

The act of deliberately engaging in fraudulent activities to artificially influence market prices, trading volumes, or other key indicators in financial markets

Maximum Sentence: £10000 Penalty

V. Miscellaneous Forbidden Acts

5.1 - Criminal Conspiracy

The act of planning and discussing a criminal act with the intent to carry it out.

Maximum Sentence: £1000 Penalty, 10 Minutes Prison

VI. Enforcement of Code

6.1 - Offence Enforcement

Persons who commit a crime involving a punishment issued per “Offence” can be executed by a law enforcement officer. If a person believes that they have been unlawfully punished by law enforcement, they have 30 days to contest the decision in court.

6.2 - Sentence Enforcement

Persons who commit a crime that involve a “Maximum Sentence” can only be punished if they are found guilty in a court of law. These punishments must be issued through judicial rulings.

6.3 - Types of Punishment

There are three main types of punishment outlined in the Harlonian criminal code. “Prison” involves detaining a person for a set period of time, “Penalty” is a fee levied against a person paid to the government, “Seizure” involves seizing a financial asset such as stolen funds or items. Courts may also issue punishments not specified within this act only if asked by the prosecution, and if they see fit. These unspecified punishments could include forfeiture of a company or property, an order of restraint, or the suspension of a licence.

6.4 - Amendments to the Code

Parliament may, through the typical means of legislative procedure, pass legislation that will add crimes to the criminal code. In order for a new crime to be considered enforceable, there must be a clear description of the act with the possible punishment outlined.
