Deposit Insurance Act

HB 3-087

Author: Westray

6 Dec 2023

Opening Declaration

  1. Currently, there is no security for deposits made in a bank, which leaves people open to the risk of losing their money if a bank defaults on debt or closes down.
  2. The bill establishes a public crown corporation to provide deposit insurance to banks and financial institutions.

Information

Corporation Powers and Responsibilities

  1. This act will hereby establish a Crown corporation known as the Harlonian Deposit Insurance Corporation (HDIC). The following requirements for it will be set out:
  1. It will be owned by the Government of Harlon at an arms-length.
  2. There will be a Chair appointed to manage the corporation.
  3. The Chair of the HDIC will report directly to the  Prime Minister and Minister of Finance regarding its operations.
  4. This corporation will be responsible for working with and regulating financial institutions to protect individual deposits against the failure of a financial institution.
  1. The HDIC will be able to insure bank deposits up to £50000 per individual account holder. This will be capped at a maximum of £300000 per financial institution, unless otherwise approved in a special budgetary request through parliament.

Requirements to Obtain Coverage

  1. If a financial institution wishes to obtain and maintain insurance coverage for deposits, they are required to:
  1. Provide a list of the amount of funds stored in accounts upon request.
  2. Take record of all financial transactions (withdrawals, deposits, etc.)
  3. Ensure they are a registered business under the Business Registry Act.
  4. Ensure they are a registered financial institution, verified by Harlonian Staff.
  5. Remain transparent with the Chair of the HDIC.
  1. Once a financial institution obtains insurance coverage for deposits, they are considered “HDIC Certified” and will continue to be protected unless they do not abide by the requirements in the above subsection (Information §2)
  1. The Chair of the HDIC is not permitted to discriminate against or unjustly remove certified protected status from any institution. If insurance coverage is removed, then a reason within the law must be given.
  2. If a financial institution wishes to challenge the decision of the HDIC, they can take it to court. If the court believes that the removal of insurance coverage was not justified, then they may reinstate it by judicial order.

Using the Insurance

  1. In the event that an institution defaults on debt, closes down, or is unable to pay its depositors, the HDIC will step in to provide assistance to all account holders.
  2. The financial institution must use all existing capital, assets, and funds to pay off account holders first, before the HDIC offers any reimbursement.

Requirements

  1. This act will require the government to appoint an individual to manage the HDIC as its Chair. This individual is not restricted to any requirement, and may also be a member of the current government if necessary.
  2. This act will require discretionary power be given to the HDIC regarding government funds beyond the regular budget process.
  3. Additionally, this act will require the government to enforce regulations upon financial institutions in accordance with the law.

Implementation and timeline

  1. This act will be implemented within one week after passage.
